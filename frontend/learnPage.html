What is Medical Cannabis?

It is the use of cannabis plant types Sativa, Indica or hybrids of these plants and their cannabinoids (such as THC, CBD, etc.) as a palliative treatment alternative for certain debilitating conditions.
 
Benefits:
  • Helps reduce pain, inflammation, and problems related to muscle control
  • Palliative treatment with an analgesic and anti-inflammatory effect
  • Reduces symptoms of chronic diseases
  • Help against insomnia
  • Relieves pain

    Patients

    As provided in Regulation 9038, a patient is a person who receives a recommendation from a licensed doctor for Medicinal Cannabis as a treatment for their condition and who has been issued an identification by the Board or Office after the registration process, in accordance with the Law No. 42-2017 and Regulation 9038. To see more information, click the button below.
     

     
     

    Doctors

    Regulation 9038 defines an Authorized Doctor as a person licensed in Puerto Rico authorized to practice the profession of medicine, who has a license to prescribe and/or administer drugs in accordance with state and federal laws and regulations, and who also meets the requirements established in Law No. 42-2017 and additional Regulations and requirements.     Licenses

    Since medical cannabis is legalized in Puerto Rico, there are multiple regulations to oversee the industry, including different types of licenses for the use, cultivation, manufacture, distribution, and sale of medical cannabis.
